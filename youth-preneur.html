Nikhil Sahai

Associate Director, Data Insights and Analytics

  • In today’s hyper-competitive environment, young start-ups often focus on building their product, acquiring users, and securing funding. While these are essential, one critical pillar often gets overlooked: data.

    As someone who has worked with start-ups in their early stages, I’ve seen firsthand how the right data setup can make or break a company’s ability to scale. Data isn’t just for mature companies; start-ups that establish solid data foundations early on gain an edge in decision-making, growth, and investor confidence.

    The Problem: Start-ups Often Treat Data as an Afterthought

    For many early-stage start-ups, data feels like something to worry about “later.” Founders may assume they’re too small to need proper data systems, or they’re overwhelmed by the sheer number of tools and technologies available. As a result, critical data often gets siloed, disorganized, or ignored altogether. By the time they need insights to scale, they’re scrambling to clean up the mess.

    This reactive approach can cost start-ups valuable time, resources, and opportunities.

    The Solution: Start Small, but Start Smart

    Here’s the good news: setting up your start-up’s data doesn’t need to be complex or expensive. By prioritizing the basics, you can build a foundation that will grow with you. Here are a few actionable steps to get started:

    1. Define Your Key Metrics (North Star and KPIs)

    • Focus on the metrics that matter most to your business. For example:
      • Acquisition (e.g., number of new users)
      • Retention (e.g., user churn or engagement)
      • Revenue (e.g., MRR or ARR)
    • Having a clear North Star metric ensures everyone is aligned toward a common goal.

    2. Organize Your Data Tools and Sources

    • At the minimum, set up tools for:
      • Data Collection: Google Analytics, Mixpanel, or Segment for user behavior.
      • CRM and Sales: HubSpot or Salesforce to track customer relationships.
      • Data Storage: Google Sheets, Airtable, or BigQuery to keep things organized.

    3. Start Building Dashboards

    Even a simple dashboard (using tools like Looker Studio, Tableau, or Power BI) can give you visibility into your key metrics. Dashboards help you:

    • Spot trends early
    • Understand what’s working (and what’s not)
    • Make data-backed decisions, not guesses

    4. Build a Culture of Data

    Encourage every team member to think about data. Data-driven decision-making should be baked into your culture from day one. Ask questions like:

    • “What does the data say?”
    • “What are we tracking, and why?”

    Real-Life Impact: How Strong Data Systems Empower Start-ups

    When start-ups implement a solid data setup early, the results can be transformative. Here are a few benefits I’ve seen in action:

    • Faster Decision-Making: Leaders can pivot quickly based on clear insights rather than gut feelings.
    • Smarter Growth: By tracking acquisition channels and user behavior, start-ups optimize their marketing spend and scale efficiently.
    • Investor Confidence: Investors love start-ups that know their numbers. Having clean, organized data signals maturity and readiness for growth.

    Take the First Step

    Start-ups don’t need complex, enterprise-level data systems on day one. What they do need is a thoughtful, structured approach to data. I’ve worked with founders to train their teams, set up their first data pipelines, and turn raw data into actionable insights that drive growth.

    If you’re building a young company and want to start leveraging data to grow smarter, let’s connect. Getting your data right from the beginning isn’t a luxury—it’s a game-changer.
